My Journey Through Life: Marching Into the Unknown

Childhood Years

      1This is about my childhood, from the time I can barely remember. 2When I was a child, it was still a time when we played outside, enjoying different physical games like taya-tayaan, patintero, or langit lupa. 3I played with my neighbors back then, and I wouldn't even notice that night had already fallen—until my parents called me to go home. 4Most of the time, I played running games like taya-tayaan or langit lupa, and it would go on until night. 5I remember an abandoned car in our area, and we used to climb on it while playing langit lupa. 6Because of those intense games, I often got injured. 7My knees were constantly scraped, and one time, I even hurt my head when I tripped and fell. 8I was so hyper back then that whenever I saw an opportunity, I would just run for no reason at all. 9Before I started school, my days were filled with nothing but play. 10I didn't know what boredom was—every day was fun. 11But when school started, I realized I didn’t really like studying. 12I only looked forward to recess and lunch, so I could play with my classmates. 13Of course, I studied a little, but my knowledge was lacking. 14I remember that when I entered second grade, I didn’t even know what the word name meant. 15When we were asked to write our names in a book, I copied my seatmate’s answer, so instead of writing my own name, I wrote his. 16That pretty much sums up my elementary days—I didn't listen to my teachers; I just wanted to play. 17I wasn’t the kind of kid who wasn’t scared of teachers, though. 18I still feared getting scolded. 19But I remember some of my classmates would curse at teachers just for fun. 20School was hectic back then. 21Even if you wanted to listen to the lecture, you couldn’t because your seatmates or the people behind you would keep pestering you. 22Bullying was also common. 23If you didn’t want to be bullied, you had to stand your ground. 24It was so common in elementary school that I often got into fights because of it. 25You had no choice but to fight back, honestly. 26If you told your teacher about it, they would ignore it, and your bullies would pester you even more. 27But if you fought back, they would hesitate next time because they knew you weren’t a pushover. 28Even though the environment was harsh and fistfights were common, I still find those times memorable for some reason. 29After a while, playing outside wasn’t a thing for me anymore because our dad bought a PSP. 30I got addicted to that gadget, so when I was home or during summer, I would play it all day. 31At that time, we only had one PSP, so I had to share it with my brother, and sometimes we fought over who should play. 32I think that’s the reason why I enjoy playing video games today. 33That was also the time when watching movies on CDs was popular. 34It was like a family bonding activity for us—we would borrow each other's CDs after finishing a movie to save money.




Teenage Years

      1About my teenage years, this is the time that I become more conscious of my actions. 2I somehow become the shy type at this time compared to when I was much younger that I didn't care about other people. 3I become more studious at this point, and the people are much more mature, so being in a fight is rare. 4I fought someone at school, but just one time, because he kept pestering me every day. 5Like, he was at my back, and my bag was on the chair. 6After the classes were finished, I would notice that my bag had writings on it, and it also had boogers. 7I disregarded it for a few weeks and didn't do anything about it, but at some point, I just had enough. 8So, I waited for the classes to end and then pushed him suddenly, so we fist-fought until our classmates broke up the fight. 9Nothing serious happened after that, it's just that he stopped pestering me. 10Also, I got fascinated by the drummers in my school, so I joined the band to be a drummer. 11It's fun during practice, especially during the parade, where we have to drum nonstop until we get calluses on our hands. 12Unfortunately, sometimes the weather was bad, so we would only parade inside the school. 13I think that's the only extracurricular activity I did at that time, but it was fun. 14Also, in our school, there were a lot of events, so I joined many of them. 15There were some events where we had to dance, so we had to practice for it. 16We practiced after classes, either in the barangay or at someone else's house. 17Fortunately, we won a lot of competitions because of our efforts, although it was tiring since we had to walk a lot during practices, and we would finish late at night. 18During my high school days, we were always busy with projects and events, but whenever I had time, I would hang out with my friends. 19I remember our hangout place, which was the house of a friend. 20We just walked to get there, even though it was far from school. 21If we had group activities or events, we would go there to practice. 22We also played there and watched movies together—it was our tambayan when we had nothing else to do. 23Of course, there were challenges in my teenage years, like anxiety, but I was able to manage it and get through it. 24I remember that during recitations, I couldn't say a thing—my mind would go blank because I was so nervous. 25It was always like that; I would just sit there, not saying anything. 26That was the hardest thing for me, so I was really scared whenever there was a recitation or reporting. 27I would worry about it even a week before the report, so I really hated it at that time. 28Additionally, this was the time that I got addicted to video games. 29I spent my time playing Mobile Legends, both at school and during my free time at home. 30That's why I became so good at it, and I played against students from other sections and mostly won. 31It was memorable; we always played together during recess and lunch. 32Our internet connection was slow at school, but one of our classmates had a good connection, so we connected to his hotspot. 33We would just play during our free time, either together or against each other, and we would tease each other while playing. 34But unfortunately, the pandemic came, and classes were suspended. 35We didn't get to graduate on-site because of the lockdown. 36It was sad that we didn't get to say goodbye in person, but that's just how it is, I guess.




College Years

      1During my college days, this is the time that I really go out of my comfort zone. 2During my high school days, I had a friend that I always went with, and I always relied on him, so it was really hard starting college alone. 3I actually had the choice of going with him, but I thought to myself that this was the time to be independent, to go out of my comfort zone. 4So, the first day of college came, and we had to introduce ourselves. 5For some reason, I was so nervous to introduce myself, but I did it. 6I'm still adjusting to my new environment. 7I still didn't know anyone from my class, but after a while, I met my circle. 8College is still hard, but somehow not as hard as it was in senior high. 9In senior high, we had piles of schoolwork, but now in college, it's more manageable. 10But of course, it's still hard, and I still have to work hard to get a passing score from quizzes or exams. 11I always liked to cram, so I would review for it the day before, during my commute hours. 12In my first year of college, the new normal was implemented, so online classes were rare, and we really had to go to the campus. 13Unfortunately, my home is really far from school, so it would take 2 hours to commute, which made it really hard to adjust. 14Especially when there was traffic, it would take almost 3 hours, so I would be late for class. 15I had to wake up early because of that, and I would get home late. 16Sometimes I had to do homework, so I had no energy left to do anything else, and I would just sleep. 17At first, it was hard, but I got used to it. 18Luckily, I enjoyed commuting. 19I enjoyed it while listening to music and getting lost in thought. 20Sometimes, I would just sleep on the bus since it was really far, and for some reason, I never missed my destination. 21The only thing I didn't like was when there were no seats left, so I had to stand during those 2 hours. 22And the conductor would literally put a lot of people on the bus, so it was hard to stand, like there was no space left. 23We also sometimes had long breaks, so we either ate outside or stayed in the library. 24The library became our tambayan when we didn't have anything to do. 25It was always the place where we had to review. 26When I was sleepy, I just went there and slept. 27I went to the 2nd floor instead of another floor because it was quieter there. 28When I compare myself to when I was in high school or senior high, I think I've improved. 29I'm forced to go out of my comfort zone, not being with the friend I was comfortable with, and I've grown as a person. 30Now, I can do things I couldn't in the past. 31So, I'm glad that I parted ways with that friend.
