Why Visit Borealis Basecamp?

To me, Borealis Basecamp in Alaska has a dream destination feel in it. Watching the northern lights from a cozy igloo gives me this thrilling idea. I can picture the silence with countless beautiful snowflakes whenever I would imagine it somewhere in an alternate world. Although I haven't been, I would just know it would be an unforgettable experience.

How to Get There

By Air: Fly into Fairbanks International Airport (FAI), the nearest major airport.

By Transfer: Borealis Basecamp is about 25 miles north of Fairbanks. The basecamp offers shuttle services from the airport or downtown Fairbanks.

By Car: Rental cars are an option, but road conditions should be checked, especially in winter.

By Tour: Many operators provide guided aurora viewing tours that include transportation to Borealis Basecamp.

Other Attractions in Fairbanks & Alaska

In Fairbanks:

  • University of Alaska Museum of the North: Explore exhibits on Alaska’s history, culture, and wildlife.
  • Chena Hot Springs: Relax in natural hot springs, visit the ice museum, and enjoy a chance to see the Northern Lights.
  • Trans-Alaska Pipeline: See one of the world’s engineering marvels up close.

Elsewhere in Alaska:

  • Denali National Park: Home to North America’s highest peak and incredible wildlife.
  • Anchorage: Alaska’s largest city, offering cultural attractions and outdoor adventures.
  • Kenai Fjords National Park: A breathtaking coastal park with glaciers and marine wildlife.
  • Juneau: The capital city, known for Mendenhall Glacier and whale-watching tours.
